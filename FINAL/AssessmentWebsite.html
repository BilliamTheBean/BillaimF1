Introduction to F1

The start of F1

The FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile) established the F1 World Championship in 1950, with the first official race held at Silverstone, UK. The series aimed to create a standardized set of rules for the fastest, most advanced single-seater cars. The early years featured iconic manufacturers like Ferrari, Alfa Romeo, and Mercedes-Benz, setting the stage for a global motorsport phenomenon.

How F1 Works

In Formula 1, teams build advanced hybrid cars for global races. Drivers compete in Grand Prix events, aiming for the fastest lap times and overall wins. Races involve qualifying sessions and strategic elements like tire management and pit stops. Points are awarded based on race positions, contributing to drivers' and constructors' championships. High-speed action, cutting-edge technology, and tactical decisions define F1.
